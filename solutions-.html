Identity and Credentials

A key application of Emali’s technology is in the deployment of decentralised identity platforms. Traditional identity systems are engineered to maintain control over a trust domain (e.g. within a company). An administrator functions as a root of trust to onboard users into the trust domain. In exchange, users need to trust an administrator to safe keep the data and ensure the trust worthiness of other users within the trust domain. While traditional identity systems function well for intra organization activities, these identity systems will need to resolve challenges of privacy, authenticity and governance in order to support inter organization transactions. An example is the intricate SWIFT messaging system used by Banks.

Corporate Data Share

Corporate Data Share (CDS) allows data owners, data administrators and data consumers to exchange data in a compliant, privacy preserving way. Current data exchange technology and processes creates scattered data repositories managed and verified by multiple platform administrators. Data owners also lose control of their data by transferring their information to platform administrators making them dependent on data administrators to safe keep their privacy, accuracy and security of their data. Data privacy, security and export regulations are also increasingly implemented across various jurisdictions, a reflection of the shift in public awareness and policy makers exercising policy sovereignty away from global technology companies.

Enterprise Process Governance and Consent

The transparency, authenticity, auditability and provenance of any multi organization business process can be improved through the use of user controlled cryptographic keys and secure registries. Emali’s digital infrastructure can support any business process where approvals, information capture and sensitive data sharing is required. These multi organization platforms can be deployed in both DLT enabled and DLT-less environments with different governance configuration to suite various business models.

Digital Wallets and Passports

Emali’s cryptographic libraries are also deployed in white labelled wallets and passports for enterprise use. It combiles public-private key management with Sybil resistance, key recovery and API-less integration into legacy identity domains. The wallet and passport modules can support any public-private key architecture with minimal development and can be used for identity and finance use cases.
